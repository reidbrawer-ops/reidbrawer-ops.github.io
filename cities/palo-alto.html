Peninsula region

Palo Alto

One park does almost all the work here. Mitchell Park's 15 courts make it the single biggest dedicated pickleball flagship on the Peninsula, and the home base of a 750-member nonprofit club that's been organizing play for years.

15 courts 1 flagship park

Where to play

Mitchell Park

600 E Meadow Dr, Palo Alto, CA 94303 Directions →
15 outdoor hard courts 8 permanent + 7 multi-purpose Free

Fifteen courts total: eight are permanent pickleball courts open 24/7, the other seven are multi-purpose and open 8 a.m.–3 p.m. daily. Six of the fifteen have lights for night play. Drop-in and free — when courts are full, stack two or four paddles by the net and rotate in.

  • Mitchell Park is home base for the Palo Alto Pickleball Club, a nonprofit with over 750 members that organizes regular play and events.
  • The paddle-stack rotation is the local etiquette — place your paddles at the net in the order you arrived.
  • Because nearly all Peninsula demand funnels into one park, expect it busy most evenings and weekend mornings.

Sources: Palo Alto Pickleball Club · City of Palo Alto — Mitchell Park. Verified July 2026 — always confirm hours before a special trip.
