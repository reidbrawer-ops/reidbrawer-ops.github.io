Peninsula region

Palo Alto

One park does almost all the work here. Mitchell Park's 15 courts make it the Peninsula's biggest pickleball flagship — and home base for a 750-member nonprofit club that's organized play for years.

15 courts 1 flagship park

Where to play

Mitchell Park

600 E Meadow Dr, Palo Alto, CA 94303 Directions →
15 outdoor hard courts 8 permanent + 7 multi-purpose Free

Eight are permanent pickleball courts open 24/7; the other seven are multi-purpose, open 8 a.m.–3 p.m. daily. Six of the fifteen have lights for night play. Drop-in and free — when courts are full, stack two or four paddles by the net and rotate in.

  • Mitchell Park is home base for the Palo Alto Pickleball Club, a 750-member nonprofit that organizes regular play and events.
  • The paddle-stack rotation is the local etiquette — place your paddles at the net in the order you arrived.
  • Because nearly all Peninsula demand funnels into one park, expect it busy most evenings and weekend mornings.

Sources: Palo Alto Pickleball Club · City of Palo Alto — Mitchell Park. Verified July 2026 — always confirm hours before a special trip.
