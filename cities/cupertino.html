South Bay region

Cupertino

Memorial Park is still the anchor: free, first-come-first-served outdoor play, with a city-mandated quiet-paddle policy to keep the neighbors sane. This round also turned up Cupertino Sports Center's indoor sessions — a more reliable bet than the Sports Center's own outdoor courts — and De Anza College's outdoor courts nearby. Court counts are disputed at more than one venue here, so read the notes before you drive over expecting a specific number.

Court count is disputed: a direct places2play fetch (confirmed on two independent attempts) says 4 courts, while most search-snippet sources say 8 — this page treats 4 as the more reliable figure but flags that 8 shows up elsewhere. Nets are portable, set up on what's described as either existing tennis lines or permanent pickleball lines depending on the source — safest description is portable nets on existing tennis lines. Free and cannot be reserved. Organized open play runs Tuesdays 10am–1pm; general park pickleball is allowed from daylight to 9pm per one source. Quiet-category paddles and balls are requested dawn–9am and 7–9pm.

One indoor and six outdoor courts. The outdoor courts have no currently scheduled drop-in events per source, so the indoor sessions are the more reliable option here. Drop-in isn't required to show up, but rentals and lessons can be booked via CourtReserve. Fee is $6 for non-members.

Good to know

A San José Spotlight report described Cupertino as a "pickleball desert" — demand from local players, including the city's large tech workforce, outpaces the courts available relative to the city's size. If you're used to court counts elsewhere in the South Bay, expect Cupertino's options to feel tighter.
