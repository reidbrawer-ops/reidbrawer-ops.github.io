Redwood City

Red Morton Park is still the one stop in Redwood City, but it's really two venues in one complex: four lighted outdoor courts that anyone can walk onto, and three indoor courts inside the Senior Center building next door with a single weekly window. Across town, the Sequoia YMCA runs a small beginner-welcome indoor session twice a week.

Four lighted outdoor courts in a big shared park complex that also has a skatepark, tennis courts, soccer fields, and basketball courts. Open daily from dawn to dusk, with the lighted courts playable until 10 p.m. Secondary sources describe this as public drop-in, first-come-first-served play, but the city's own pickleball page didn't load on this research pass, so whether Redwood City's ActiveCommunities system offers a reservation option for these outdoor courts specifically wasn't directly confirmed — worth a call before assuming it's walk-up only.

A separate indoor venue inside the Senior Center building at the same park complex as the outdoor courts above — three indoor courts, one narrow window a week (Wednesdays only), and a nominal $1 fee at the door.
