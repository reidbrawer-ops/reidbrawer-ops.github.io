Los Altos

Three listed venues, but only one is a straightforward public option: McKenzie Park's six shared outdoor courts. The other two are membership-gated — a private country club, and a school gym program whose outdoor-court claim isn't confirmed by the city.

Los Altos' only outdoor public pickleball venue: six courts painted onto two existing tennis courts at McKenzie Park, with portable nets supplied by the city. Pickleball gets priority Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Saturday (6am–10pm); tennis has priority the other three days, and city-sponsored tennis lessons can bump either. Play itself is free, though the city's separate tennis-court reservation fee ($10/hr resident, $8/hr nonprofit) may or may not apply here — that wasn't clearly resolved in city documentation.

Four dedicated, permanently lined outdoor courts with LED lights, part of a private country club. Membership requires sponsorship by four current Proprietary members plus an application and initiation fee; there's no separately published per-visit pickleball charge. The club's own site says courts are lit for member play until 10pm, seven days a week, though one third-party aggregator lists more limited hours (weekdays 9am–5pm, weekends 12–8pm) that don't match — the club's own claim is treated as more reliable here.

The city's own page describes only an indoor gym program here: membership-gated pickleball in the Egan Junior High gym, Monday and Wednesday evenings, 6:30–9:30pm. Pickleheads separately lists this location as 8 total courts (4 indoor, 4 outdoor), but the city's site never mentions outdoor courts at this campus, and other aggregators disagree with each other too (one says 6 total, another 4) — treat the outdoor claim as unconfirmed, especially since this is an active school campus. Annual Rec membership runs $96 resident / $115 non-resident (per the city), with a free 1-month trial; non-member drop-in play ended June 1, 2024.
