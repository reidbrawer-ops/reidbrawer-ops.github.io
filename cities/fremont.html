Cities / East Bay / Fremont

Fremont

Fremont's anchor is the free, dedicated Knoll at Central Park; the Fremont Tennis Center adds seven reservable courts, plus a couple of smaller membership and drop-in spots. Citywide, Fremont runs to roughly 11 locations and 55 courts — more than we've profiled here, so treat this list as a curated starting point, not the full count.

8 free dedicated courts at the Knoll 7 reservable courts at the Tennis Center $5–7/hr at the Tennis Center

Fremont's biggest dedicated setup — 8 outdoor acrylic courts with permanent lines and nets, free and first-come-first-served. A paddle-in rotation keeps play fair when busy, especially on Sundays. Morning players on the west-end courts face direct sun and glare. Note: you'll sometimes see this address given as 40000 Paseo Padre Pkwy — that's the Tennis Center below, not the Knoll.

Fremont Tennis Center

40000 Paseo Padre Pkwy, Fremont, CA 94538 Directions ↗
7 outdoor courts Reservation required $5/hr residents, $7/hr non-residents
SurfaceHigh — concrete, dedicated lines, permanent nets
LevelNot specified
WeatherInland Tri-Valley-adjacent — hot, sunny, especially summer afternoons
WaitLow — reservation system controls access

Seven courts total: 3 regulation courts striped onto 60' junior tennis courts, plus 4 more on a converted 78' tennis court — all with dedicated lines and permanent nets, reservable through the City of Fremont rather than first-come-first-served.
