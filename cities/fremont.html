Fremont

Fremont's anchor is still the free, dedicated Knoll at Central Park, but it isn't the only real option: the Fremont Tennis Center adds seven reservable courts, and a couple of smaller membership and drop-in spots round things out. A search-snippet summary puts the citywide total near 11 locations and 55 courts — more than we could individually confirm here, so treat this list as a strong starting point rather than exhaustive.

Fremont's biggest and most established dedicated setup — 8 outdoor courts, acrylic surface, permanent lines and nets, free and first-come-first-served. A paddle-in rotation keeps play fair when it's busy, and it does get "bustling," per usage data, most of all on Sundays. Morning players on the west-end courts face direct sun and glare, so plan around that if you're heat-sensitive. Note: some sources list this address as 40000 Paseo Padre Pkwy — that's actually the separate Fremont Tennis Center below, not the Knoll.

Fremont Tennis Center

7 outdoor courts Reservation required $5/hr residents, $7/hr non-residents

WaitLow — reservation system controls access

Seven courts total: 3 regulation pickleball courts striped onto 60' junior tennis courts, plus 4 more on a converted 78' tennis court. All dedicated lines with permanent nets, and all reservable through the City of Fremont rather than first-come-first-served.
