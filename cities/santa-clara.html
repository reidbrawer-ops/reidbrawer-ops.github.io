South Bay region

Santa Clara

Two reservation-only venues, no walk-up option at either. The Community Recreation Center at Central Park is the public route — city-run rates, a phone check-in system, and courts shared with tennis. Bay Club Santa Clara is the private route — a members-only club with dedicated pickleball lines and a reputation as one of the busier scenes in the city.

Six outdoor hard courts with lines blended into the existing tennis courts, portable nets, city-run reservation system open to residents and non-residents alike. Open play drop-in runs $6 residents/$7 non-residents; reserved courts run $8/hr residents, $10/hr non-residents. You must check in by phone at 408-247-0178 or in person before heading to your assigned court — this isn't a walk-up venue. Class tiers ("New Learners & Beginners 0–1.5," "Advanced Beginner 2.0–2.5") are lesson levels, not requirements for open play. Hours vary noticeably by day, so check before you go: Monday and Wednesday run latest, at 9:30 p.m.

A private club rather than a public facility — membership required, though guest memberships are available. Court count depends on the source: most consistently cited at 6, with some listings claiming as many as 10, so treat that upper figure with caution. Lines are dedicated to pickleball rather than shared with tennis, and lighted for evening play. The listed programmed times (Monday/Wednesday/Friday evenings, Saturday late morning) are scheduled pickleball sessions, not the full facility's open hours. It's described as one of the most popular pickleball scenes in the city, so expect the programmed sessions to be busy.

  • Neither Santa Clara venue is walk-up — the Community Recreation Center requires a phone or in-person check-in, and Bay Club requires membership.
  • If you're not a Bay Club member, the Community Recreation Center is the only way to play in Santa Clara proper without joining a club.
  • Bay Club's programmed pickleball windows are narrower than its overall facility hours — call 408-738-2582 before assuming you can walk on outside those times.
