North Bay region

San Rafael

Marin's pickleball scene runs indoor-first and membership-heavy: Flyte Racquet Club (which absorbed the old Marin Indoor Pickleball brand) has by far the most courts, Pueblo Park is the county's one free dedicated outdoor court, and a handful of smaller options round things out. Smaller scene than the South Bay, but far easier to book.

Formerly branded Marin Indoor Pickleball (est. 2022) at this same address — now Flyte Racquet Club, with 23 total pickleball courts, the largest count in the county by a wide margin. Full membership runs $2,500 (individual) or $3,500 (family) initiation, with no court fees and 90 minutes/day per sport once you're in. Lower-commitment monthly passes also exist and are a cheaper way in than full membership. It's unclear whether the old pay-per-play, no-membership booking option survived the rebrand — call ahead to check.

Marin County's first outdoor court built specifically for pickleball — one court, no formal booking system. Getting a game together is informal: you typically need to round up a foursome, with regular play often landing Monday/Wednesday/Friday mornings 8–9am. Free, but plan on coordinating rather than just showing up.

Good to know

Marin's scene is small and membership-heavy compared to the South Bay or Peninsula — but that means it's realistic to book a same-week reservation here in a way it often isn't in San Jose or Palo Alto. The Albert J. Boro Community Center's old indoor drop-in program is discontinued as of March 2024 — don't plan around it.

  • Flyte Racquet Club and Marin Indoor Pickleball are the same physical location under different branding — don't look for both separately.
  • Pueblo Park requires rounding up your own group; it isn't a reliable walk-up option.
  • Court counts and access rules at the smaller venues above weren't independently reconfirmed this pass — call ahead before a special trip.
