Alameda

Six venues turn up in Alameda listings, but two carry real question marks: Alameda Point Gym's own city facility page never mentions pickleball, and South Shore Apartments' rooftop courts appear to be a private amenity with no confirmed public access. The other four are solid — free city courts at Lincoln Park and Leydecker Park, plus two membership clubs, The Hub Sports Club and Harbor Bay Club, with dedicated indoor and outdoor courts.

The best-documented pickleball venue in Alameda — the city's own park page explicitly lists a "Pickle Ball Court" feature, and the four courts were resurfaced specifically for pickleball in 2017 with dedicated lines and nets. Access is by keypad code rather than an open gate; contact ARPD's Recreation Supervisor (510-747-7555) for the current code. Free and walk-up, though the Alameda Pickleball Club runs several weekly sessions here — Monday/Wednesday midday, Wednesday/Saturday mornings, and Saturday afternoon.

Tennis courts on Bay Farm Island shared with pickleball via portable nets, not dedicated pickleball-only courts — though the city publishes a dedicated "Pickleball Net Code" access document, confirming the sport is officially supported here. Free and walk-up, dawn to dusk. Sources disagree on the street number: the city's own page says 3221 Mecartney Road (used here), while Google Maps and other aggregators say 3225 — worth double-checking if you're relying on GPS.

The largest pickleball facility in Alameda by far — 15 total courts, 8 indoor on a PPA-approved cushioned surface plus 7 outdoor, all dedicated with permanent lines and nets. It's a paid club, not a public park: Gold Fitness + Pickleball membership runs $135/month plus a $69 initiation fee, though non-members can drop in for $35 when space allows. Opened May 2024 with lighted outdoor courts, and it's the home venue of Major League Pickleball's Bay Area Breakers.

Membership pricing (Individual/Couple/Family/Senior tiers) isn't published online — the club asks that you call or submit an inquiry form.

Real conflict here: Pickleheads and another aggregator list this as a 2-court indoor pickleball venue, but the City of Alameda's own facility page for this exact building tags it only "Gymnasium" — basketball, volleyball, restrooms — with no pickleball mention, and none of its 30 Google reviews (mostly basketball, jiu-jitsu, and wrestling) mention pickleball either. If pickleball happens here at all, it's likely an occasional portable-net setup on the gym floor rather than a standing program. Worth a call to ARPD (510-747-7555) before making a special trip.

A real amenity — a 1-acre rooftop "Sports Park" at this Greystar-managed apartment complex, with four pickleball courts (permanent lines, portable nets) and three skill-tiered leagues (beginner, intermediate, advanced) run through MyOPL.net. But it's a private complex: Google categorizes the whole property as an apartment rental agency, none of its 155 reviews mention pickleball, and no source confirms whether the courts or leagues are open to non-residents. Treat this one as tentative — confirm access with the leasing office before counting on it.
