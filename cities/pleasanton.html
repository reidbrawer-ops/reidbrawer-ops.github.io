Pleasanton

Pleasanton's scene centers on Muirwood Community Park — the largest dedicated pickleball facility in the Tri-Valley — backed up by a shared-line tennis park, a Saturday-friendly indoor gym, and a couple of private clubs for members. The Tri-Valley Pickleball Club is a good local resource for current schedules and open play.

Described as "the largest dedicated pickleball facility in the Tri-Valley" — 6 dedicated outdoor courts, free and first-come-first-served, can't be reserved. Opened November 2022 and draws 40 or more players across all skill and age groups on a typical weekend, with a rotating-court system to keep access fair when it fills up. The most positive crowd sentiment of Pleasanton's public options, even with the Saturday rush.

Six indoor courts split between recreational and competitive play. No reservation needed for individual play, but entry requires a pass: $4.50 for residents, $5.25 for non-residents. Buy multi-day passes ahead at pleasantonfun.com, or single-day passes in person at the Community Services Dept, 200 Old Bernal Ave (no cash accepted on-site). Contact: Rachel Prater, 925-931-3443.
