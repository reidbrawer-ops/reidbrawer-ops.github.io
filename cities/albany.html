Cities / East Bay / Albany

Albany

Ocean View Park is Albany's active pickleball venue — four dedicated outdoor courts, free to walk up or reservable ahead. Albany High School also has six indoor courts, but their current operating status is unclear.

Four dedicated outdoor courts with permanent lines and nets, free for walk-up play or reservable ahead for $11/hour resident, $13/hour non-resident (you may still see lower rates quoted from an older fee schedule, so confirm before booking). The city runs Tuesday Outdoor Pickleball Open Play 4–6pm, and BORP hosts a weekly adaptive pickleball program here (Thursdays and Saturdays) open to players of any skill level. It sometimes gets filed under Berkeley, but this is an Albany city park — Buchanan Street runs along the border, which is the likely source of the mixup.

Six indoor wood courts with permanent lines painted on the gym floor — bring your own net, since none is fixed in place. There's a one-time fee to play, but the only figure on record is an old one: $5 entry plus $2 paddle rental for a Sunday drop-in session back in 2020. That same 2020 record noted the gym was closed for maintenance, and it's unclear whether play ever resumed — Albany's recreation department lists only Ocean View Park, not this venue. Call ahead to confirm it's actually running before making a special trip.

  • Ocean View Park is reservable ahead via Albany's WebTrac portal ($11/hr resident, $13/hr non-resident) — or just walk up and play for free.
  • BORP runs a weekly adaptive pickleball program at Ocean View Park (Thursdays 5–6:30pm, Saturdays 1–4pm), open to players and volunteers of any age or skill level.
  • The city's Outdoor Pickleball Open Play runs Tuesdays 4–6pm at Ocean View Park, player-led with no instructor.
  • Albany High School's status is unclear — call Albany Recreation & Community Services (510-524-9283) or the school (510-558-2500) before a special trip.
