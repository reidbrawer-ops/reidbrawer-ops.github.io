San Leandro

Two real options: Washington Manor Park's six free, city-run courts in the flats, and the private, membership-only Bay-O-Vista Swim and Tennis Club with eight more up in the hills near Lake Chabot.

San Leandro's only city-run pickleball venue: six free outdoor courts with dedicated, permanent lines and nets, corroborated by the city's own page, Pickleheads, and four other independent aggregators — the best-confirmed venue in this guide. Courts 1, 3, and 5 can be reserved online through San Leandro Recreation; courts 2, 4, and 6 are first-come, first-served, with a 4-on/4-off rotation when players are waiting. Automatic lighting keeps it playable until 8pm daily. The property also includes two separate tennis courts and the San Leandro Family Aquatic Center, neither part of the pickleball setup.

A private membership club in the hills near Lake Chabot. Pickleheads, whoplays.us, and the club's own site (whose photo caption cites "eight courts") agree on 8 outdoor courts with dedicated, permanent nets — but one outlier, Pickleballify, claims just 4 courts with bring-your-own portable nets and a one-time fee instead of membership, a claim its own page header contradicts (it separately says "1 Pickleball Court in San Leandro"); we're leaving it unresolved rather than picking a side. Whether the courts are lit is also unresolved — Pickleheads-derived listings count lights as an amenity, while CourtSource states flatly there are none. Published hours conflict too: the club's own site lists Mon–Fri 5:30am–9:30pm and Sat–Sun 6am–9pm, while CourtSource lists much later 10am or 11:30am openings, possibly general club hours vs. pickleball-specific windows — membership is required either way, with no public dues listed, so call ahead.

  • Washington Manor Park courts 1, 3, and 5 can be reserved online through San Leandro Recreation; courts 2, 4, and 6 are first-come, first-served.
  • Bay-O-Vista Swim and Tennis Club is members and guests only — dues aren't publicly listed, so contact the club directly before visiting.
  • Heath Park, also in San Leandro, has tennis courts only per the city's own facilities page (4 tennis courts, 2 under renovation) — no pickleball there despite occasionally showing up in searches.
  • A third possible venue, Roosevelt Elementary School, is claimed by exactly one aggregator and isn't corroborated by Pickleheads, the city, the school itself, or any other source checked — we left it out; call San Leandro Recreation (510-577-3462) if you want to chase it down.
