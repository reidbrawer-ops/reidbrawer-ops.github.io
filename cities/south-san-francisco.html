South San Francisco

South San Francisco is its own incorporated city in San Mateo County — not a neighborhood of San Francisco — with six confirmed pickleball venues totaling 20 courts. Four are free, walk-up outdoor parks and two are indoor pay-to-play facilities; a couple of addresses below are genuinely unresolved between sources, and we've said so rather than guessing.

The best-corroborated venue in South San Francisco — Pickleheads, Bounce, and three independent pickleball-locator sites all agree on four outdoor courts with permanent lines, free and walk-up. Bring your own net; the city doesn't provide one. Restrooms, a picnic shelter, and grills are on site. One source lists the address as 2380 Galway Dr rather than 2350 — a minor discrepancy, not treated as a real conflict.

Two outdoor hard courts with permanent lines, bring-your-own net, free and walk-up. Naming and address are unresolved: Pickleheads and Bounce both call this "Stonegate Park" at 630 Stonegate Dr, while the city's own facility map lists a matching tennis court under a different name — "Stonegate Tennis Courts" — on Ridgeview Court, with no mention of pickleball at all. Both streets sit inside the same small Stonegate Terrace development near San Bruno Mountain State Park, so this is likely one facility described two ways rather than two separate courts, but that isn't confirmed. Treat the exact address as unverified until checked in person.

Four indoor hard courts at the city-run gym, with portable nets on permanent lines. The city's own Sports page — the more current source — lists drop-in open play Tuesdays and Thursdays, noon–3pm, $4 for adults and $2 for youth and seniors, registered through the city's online catalog. A third-party aggregator instead describes a separate Monday evening session, 7–9pm, flat $4, with a house coach for beginners; that may be a different program or simply outdated, and this page defaults to the city's own page as the primary source.

A private indoor facility with two dedicated pickleball courts on concrete, permanent nets, and a day-pass system bookable online. Existence, address, and indoor setup are confirmed by BASH's own website and an active social presence, but the court count and concrete surface come only from Pickleheads — no second source independently verifies that number. Day passes reportedly run 8am–10pm; the exact price isn't listed anywhere checked, so call or check the booking site directly. Locker rooms, a pro shop, and water are on site.

  • Orange Memorial Park's six-court pickleball project is funded and designed, but as of this writing it's still in design and bidding — the city's own project page targets construction around mid-2026, so it's not a real option yet despite showing up on a few other directories.
  • All four outdoor parks are free, walk-up, and take no reservations — arrival time matters more than planning ahead.
  • South San Francisco sits in a fog-and-wind corridor between SFO and the coastal hills — expect marine layer even in summer at the outdoor courts.
  • "Bay Club South San Francisco Tennis and Pickleball" is not actually in South San Francisco — its own listing places it in Daly City, so it's excluded from this page.
