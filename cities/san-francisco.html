San Francisco

Twenty-two locations citywide, from brand-new dedicated complexes to a single court dual-striped onto a volleyball court. Most run through one booking system; only three have permanent nets — bring your own everywhere else.

How SF's reservation system works

SF Rec & Park runs a citywide booking system through rec.us. A $5/hour fee applies at 28 of the city's 66 pickleball/tennis court locations; the rest are free. Reservations open 7 days ahead at 8:00 a.m. (a few sites open 2 days ahead at noon), you must check in within 15 minutes of your start time, and each person is capped at 1 court per day and 3 per week. Larsen and Louis Sutter's free, no-reservation courts sit outside this system entirely.

  • Reservations cap at 1 court per day and 3 per week per person across the rec.us system.
  • Only Larsen Playground, Louis Sutter Playground, and Goldman Tennis Center have permanent nets; everywhere else uses portable nets that may or may not be provided on-site.
  • Wind is a real variable — afternoon games near the coast (Sunset, Parkside, Presidio) get gusty fast, while McLaren Park and downtown courts run noticeably calmer.
