San Francisco region

San Francisco

Twenty locations citywide, from brand-new dedicated complexes to a single court dual-striped onto a volleyball court. Most run through one citywide booking system. Only three locations have permanent nets you don't have to bring yourself.

How SF's reservation system works

SF Rec & Park runs a citywide booking system through rec.us. A $5/hour fee applies at 28 of the city's 66 pickleball/tennis court locations; the rest are free. Reservations generally open 7 days ahead at 8:00 a.m. (a few sites open 2 days ahead at noon instead), you must check in within 15 minutes of your start time, and each person is capped at 1 court per day and 3 per week. Free, no-reservation courts like Larsen and Louis Sutter sit outside this system entirely.

  • Reservations are capped at 1 court per day and 3 per week per person across the rec.us system — plan around that if you're playing often.
  • Only Larsen Playground, Louis Sutter Playground, and Goldman Tennis Center have permanent pickleball nets; everywhere else uses portable nets that may or may not be provided on-site.
