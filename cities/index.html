Public pickleball venues verified against each city's own listing, organized the way you'd actually drive the loop — San Francisco, down the Peninsula, across the South Bay, up through the East Bay, over to Marin (and Vallejo).

Jump to a city

San Francisco

One city, but a rec department building faster than almost anywhere else in the Bay.

San Francisco

→

New dedicated complexes in the Sunset and Portola, lined courts in Presidio Heights, and indoor play in four rec centers when the fog rolls in.

Dedicated complexes + indoor rec centers

Peninsula

From a 750-member club at a 15-court flagship to smaller, two-park neighborhood scenes a short drive away.

Palo Alto

→

Mitchell Park's 15 courts make it the single biggest flagship on the Peninsula — eight open 24/7, six lit for nights.

Mitchell Park · 15 courts

Menlo Park

→

Eight courts split across two parks — Kelly Park, half reservable, and Nealon Park, drop-in only and the busier of the two.

Two parks · drop-in + reservable

Redwood City

→

Red Morton Park's outdoor courts, its own indoor Senior Center session, and a beginner-friendly YMCA night across town.

Outdoor park + indoor sessions

San Mateo

→

A busy downtown park with a crowding petition to its name, plus a Sunday indoor drop-in across town.

Downtown park + Sunday indoor

Belmont

→

Two small parks, four courts total — Alexander Park and Hallmark Park, both free and city-run.

Two free city parks

Burlingame

→

Free striped park courts to a 6-court indoor club, anchored by Washington Park's 8.

Park courts + indoor club

Daly City

→

A foggy community-center gym to a Bay Club outpost — thinner data than most, worth a call ahead.

Community gym + Bay Club

San Bruno

→

A pilot program painted onto City Park's tennis courts, plus a Wednesday indoor drop-in at the Rec and Aquatic Center.

Park pilot + indoor drop-in

San Carlos

→

Highlands Park's 4 free courts are the anchor; the indoor Youth Center program is paused for summer.

Highlands Park · free courts

South San Francisco

→

From Sellick Park to a members-only indoor club — a dedicated 6-court park is still in design.

Parks + members-only club

South Bay

The highest court count anywhere in the Bay Area — and its most contentious siting fight.

San Jose

→

The Bay's biggest scene by far — from a club-run park to free college courts, spread right across the city.

The Bay's biggest scene

Santa Clara

→

A public reservation court at Central Park and a private dedicated-line club at Bay Club — no walk-up option at either.

Reservation court + private club

Sunnyvale

→

The Tennis Center's dedicated courts, plus a once-a-week indoor session at the Community Center.

Tennis Center + indoor session

Cupertino

→

Memorial Park's free courts, plus a sports center and a community college with their own scenes — still called a "pickleball desert" locally.

Park + sports center + college

Mountain View

→

3 dedicated + 6 shared courts at Rengstorff Park, plus a residents-only indoor pavilion. A Cuesta Park expansion collapsed under neighborhood opposition.

Rengstorff Park · dedicated + shared

Campbell

→

A 20-court membership club (The HUB) and a seasonal city-gym drop-in are the only two options.

Membership club + city gym

Los Altos

→

McKenzie Park's 6 free courts, a private country club, and a disputed school-gym claim.

Free park + private clubs

Los Gatos

→

A private club, a JCC, a contentious free park mid-renovation, and a school gym.

Club, JCC, park + school gym

Milpitas

→

A brand-new Picklr club and two still-informal park pilots.

New Picklr club + park pilots

Saratoga

→

El Quito Park and West Valley College anchor it; two private clubs round out the scene.

El Quito Park + West Valley College

East Bay

Free city parks a few miles from members-only clubs — the range is wide here.

Oakland

→

Free city parks (Bushrod, Montclair) a few miles from a private club scene (Pickle Athletics, Oakland Hills).

Free parks + private clubs

Berkeley

→

Cedar Rose Park is the real flagship here — 4 bookable dedicated courts the city's own directory used to miss entirely.

4 courts · bookable

Walnut Creek

→

Rudgear Park's 8 free courts (the busiest spot in town) plus Heather Farm's smaller reservable option.

Rudgear Park + Heather Farm

Fremont

→

Free courts at the Central Park Knoll, reservable ones at the Tennis Center, plus a couple of private options.

Free + reservable + private

Pleasanton

→

Free courts at Muirwood, blended lines at the Community Park, plus a gym session and two private clubs.

Free parks + gym + clubs

Alameda

→

Free city parks to a 15-court club that opened in 2024.

City parks + 15-court club

Albany

→

Ocean View Park's 4 courts — long miscategorized as Berkeley's on other sites — are the city's real anchor.

Ocean View Park · anchor

Concord

→

Willow Pass Community Park's 14 free courts dwarf the city's other two options.

Willow Pass Community Park

Danville

→

Osage Station Park's 2 free courts, a Town-run tennis-court program, and two private clubs.

Osage Station Park + clubs

Dublin

→

Two parks — Wallis Ranch is the city's newest and largest, opened in 2025.

Two parks · incl. Wallis Ranch

El Cerrito

→

One park, Castro Park — 6 courts converted from tennis in a 2025 renovation.

Castro Park · 6 courts

Emeryville

→

One city-run gym, Saturdays only, 18+, registration required — the whole scene.

City gym · Saturdays only

Hayward

→

A brand-new park to a Thursday senior-center drop-in.

New park + senior drop-in

Livermore

→

Anchored by May Nissen Park's 8 courts and Livermore Downs' 4.

May Nissen Park + Livermore Downs

Richmond

→

Four venues — and a 12-court waterfront facility everyone still cites online is actually closed.

Four venues · call ahead

San Leandro

→

Washington Manor Park's 6 free courts and an 8-court private swim-and-tennis club.

Free park + private club

San Ramon

→

Central Park's 4 free courts anchor a scene that also includes a disputed-count gym.

Central Park + clubs

Union City

→

One shared gym, two courts — the thinnest scene in the East Bay, and residents say so themselves.

One shared gym

North Bay

Marin's scene is small, indoor-heavy, and easy to book ahead; Vallejo, just across the water in Solano County, is grouped here too.

San Rafael

→

Flyte Racquet Club (formerly Marin Indoor Pickleball) has 23 courts if you've got a membership; Pueblo Park is the county's one free dedicated court.

23 courts · Flyte

Novato

→

Six lined courts at Hill Recreation plus two more at Pioneer Park, all first-come first-served from roughly 8 a.m. to 10 p.m.

Hill Recreation + Pioneer Park

Vallejo

→

From a lakeside park to a rebranded fitness club — call ahead.

Five venues · call ahead
