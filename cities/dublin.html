Dublin

Two city parks: Don Biddle, where tennis courts double as striped pickleball lines, and the newer Wallis Ranch Community Park, which added dedicated pickleball courts in 2025 alongside more tennis-court overlays. Play is free and mostly drop-in, with a handful of reservable slots at each.

Two tennis-court slabs, each striped with two pickleball lines — the city's own pickleball page counts that as 4 courts, while other listings, including Pickleheads, count the two physical slabs instead, so you'll see both numbers depending on the source. "Court 2" is free and drop-in at all times; "Court 1" can be reserved ahead through dublinrecguide.com, except during the sunrise–1pm window when it shifts to tennis priority. Nets are portable and city-owned — players set them up and are expected to secure them after play.

Wallis Ranch Community Park

WaitLow — the city's newest and largest pickleball venue, with the most courts to spread out demand

Dublin's newest park (opened August 2025) and the only one built with courts purpose-made for pickleball: 4 dedicated courts with permanent nets, plus 8 more striped onto the park's 4 shared tennis courts (two lines each), for 12 total. The city's reservation system only names those 4 shared tennis courts — two bookable through dublinrecguide.com outside the sunrise–1pm window, two drop-in only — and no source confirms whether the 4 dedicated pickleball-only courts follow the same rules or are entirely first-come-first-served.

  • Both parks use the same city reservation system (dublinrecguide.com, 10 days' notice) for their bookable courts; everything else is first-come-first-served.
  • Sunrise–1pm is a citywide priority-use window at both parks — tennis and pickleball alternate priority by court, so timing can matter more than which park you choose.
  • Lights are self-operated and shut off automatically at 10pm nightly at both parks.
  • No indoor or membership pickleball club operates inside Dublin itself — the Livermore-based Tri-Valley Pickleball Club lists Wallis Ranch as one of its outdoor play locations but doesn't run a separate Dublin facility.
