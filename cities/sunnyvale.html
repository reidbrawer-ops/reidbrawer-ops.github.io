Sunnyvale

Sunnyvale Tennis Center is the city's flagship — dedicated courts plus tennis courts that convert during scheduled hours, both bookable ahead. Sunnyvale Community Center offers a smaller indoor drop-in option, though its hours are due for a call-ahead check. Sources don't agree on the Tennis Center's exact court count, so treat any single number here with some caution.

Court count is genuinely disputed across sources: a direct site fetch shows 4 dedicated courts, one search snippet says 12, and other sources describe "4 dedicated plus up to 8 tennis courts converted with portable lines during scheduled pickleball hours." The safest description: 4 dedicated pickleball courts, plus additional tennis courts converted with portable lines during scheduled hours. Reservations guarantee a time slot; drop-in is also available at set times. Fees run about $8–10/hr per court for residents ($2/person with 4 players) and $10–13/hr for non-residents.

Six indoor courts, drop-in for $5/session or $45/year. Listed hours are Mondays 10am–1pm only, but one source flagged this session as possibly suspended as of a December 2020 update — status unconfirmed, so call (408) 730-7350 to verify before visiting.

  • Las Palmas Park (800 Spinosa Dr) turned up in secondary aggregators but not on the city's own site or on Pickleheads/Places2Play — unconfirmed, worth a call to the city at (408) 732-2130 before a special trip rather than treating it as certain.
  • Weekday evenings and weekends at the Tennis Center are the hardest slots to book — plan ahead if that's your only window.
