East Bay region

Walnut Creek

Walnut Creek's real hub is Rudgear Park — eight dedicated drop-in courts and home to the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club — with Heather Farm Park's smaller, paid, reservable setup as the backup. Walnut Creek sits in the inland valley, so expect warmer, sunnier conditions than the flatland cities closer to the bay.

Eight dedicated outdoor courts on a concrete/asphalt "Dura 40" surface with permanent lines and nets — the largest and most popular pickleball venue in Walnut Creek, and home base for the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club. There's no reservation system here; it's strictly first-come-first-served, and a $2 ball donation is requested. Consistently described as "busy all day long" and "often very crowded," so plan to visit early or late if you want a shorter wait. Best overall pick for size and access, but see the caveat below before you make a special trip.

Court count is genuinely disputed: one listing says 4 dedicated pickleball courts, while a facility-overview source says 2, alongside 11 lighted tennis courts on the same site — we're not picking a side. Sources also disagree on reservation policy: one says reservations are recommended, another says they aren't required at all. In practice, drop-in play is accepted and reservations are available and worth making at busy times, bookable through Lifetime Activities (reservations are forfeited after a 15-minute no-show; cancel 4+ hours ahead). Cost is $5.00 per person per 90 minutes. Lessons are available, and the facility is connected to a Walnut Creek Pickleball Club.

Worth confirming before you go: Rudgear Park closure discussion

A local news source headline suggests the city considered or discussed closing the Rudgear Park courts. We haven't seen anything confirming a closure is actually happening, and we also haven't seen it confirmed that the idea was dropped — so treat this as genuinely open rather than resolved either way. Worth confirming current status before a special trip, especially if you're coming from outside Walnut Creek.

  • Rudgear Park is the bigger, busier, free option — go early or late in the day to avoid the worst of the wait.
  • Heather Farm Park costs $5 per person per 90 minutes but gives you a reservation option if you want a guaranteed time slot.
  • Rossmoor (1001 Golden Rain Rd) has 6 courts (reportedly 3 indoor/3 outdoor) bookable via rossmoorcsd.recdesk.com — but it's a gated 55+ community: members/guests only, not general public access, so don't plan on showing up without a resident host.
  • Both Walnut Creek venues connect to the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club — a good first stop for current conditions and etiquette questions.
