Walnut Creek

Walnut Creek's hub is Rudgear Park — eight dedicated drop-in courts and home to the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club — with Heather Farm Park's smaller, paid, reservable courts as backup. The inland valley location means warmer, sunnier conditions than the flatland cities near the bay.

Eight dedicated outdoor courts on a concrete/asphalt "Dura 40" surface with permanent lines and nets — the largest, most popular venue in Walnut Creek and home to the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club. No reservation system: it's first-come-first-served, with a $2 ball donation requested. It's busy all day long and often very crowded, so visit early or late for a shorter wait. Best pick for size and access — see the caveat below first.

The court count is unsettled — plan on somewhere between 2 and 4 dedicated pickleball courts, sharing the site with 11 lighted tennis courts. Reservations aren't strictly required, but they're worth making at busy times. Drop-in play is accepted, and reservations go through Lifetime Activities (forfeited after a 15-minute no-show; cancel 4+ hours ahead). Cost is $5.00 per person per 90 minutes. Lessons are available, and the facility connects to the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club.

Worth confirming before you go: Rudgear Park closure discussion

The city has floated closing the Rudgear Park courts. There's no confirmation yet either that it's happening or that the idea was dropped — treat the status as open. Worth confirming before a special trip, especially from outside Walnut Creek.

  • Rudgear Park is the bigger, busier, free option — go early or late to avoid the worst of the wait.
  • Heather Farm Park costs $5 per person per 90 minutes but offers a reservation option for a guaranteed time slot.
  • Rossmoor (1001 Golden Rain Rd) has 6 courts (3 indoor/3 outdoor) bookable via rossmoorcsd.recdesk.com — but it's a gated 55+ community, members/guests only, so don't plan on showing up without a resident host.
  • Both Walnut Creek venues connect to the Walnut Creek Pickleball Club — a good first stop for current conditions and etiquette questions.
