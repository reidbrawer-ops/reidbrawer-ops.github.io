Novato

Two real options, both free and first-come-first-served: Hill Recreation's six courts, and a smaller pair of courts at Pioneer Park. Data on Novato is thinner than most cities in this guide — we're keeping this page short rather than padding it.

Where to play

Hill Recreation Pickleball Courts

Top pickBeginner-friendly
500 Crescent Drive, Novato, CA 94949 Directions →
6 dedicated courts Level: All levels Hours: 8am–10pm daily
SurfaceHigh — dedicated, permanent lines/nets
LevelAll levels welcome
WeatherMild, marine-influenced — less fog than the coast
WaitHigh — reportedly one of Novato's most popular spots, no reservation option

Six dedicated courts behind Novato High, expandable to eight if demand needs it, with a paddle-rack rotation once every court is full. Free, first-come-first-served, no way to reserve ahead. An older listing under the name "Thigpen Courts" cites shorter hours and a $3 Saturday fee — that looks stale next to the more current 8am–10pm daily description, but worth a call if you're planning around it.

A smaller, quieter alternative to Hill Recreation. Free, first-come-first-served, no lights so no evening play. One aggregator's claim of indoor courts at this address conflicts with the city's own facility directory — treat that as likely an error.

  • Neither venue takes reservations — arrival time matters more than planning ahead.
  • Data on Novato's pickleball scene is thinner than most cities in this guide; confirm current status with the city (415-899-8290) before a special trip.
