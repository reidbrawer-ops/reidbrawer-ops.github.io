Milpitas

Milpitas' longest-running pickleball spots are two city parks running a nominally "temporary" pilot program since 2022, plus a multi-sport gym and a small reservable indoor court. That changed abruptly when The Picklr, a 16-court indoor club, opened at McCarthy Ranch the day before this page was researched — more than doubling the city's total courts overnight.

These aren't dedicated pickleball courts — the city striped 6 pickleball lines onto 2 of Hall Memorial Park's 4 tennis courts under a "temporary" one-year pilot program that began in August 2022. Four of the six lines are shared-use with tennis (pickleball gets priority, but tennis can still play on them); the other two are pickleball-exclusive. Whether the pilot becomes permanent is still unresolved — Milpitas Pickleball Club's advocacy page cites a May 2025 city council meeting that left Hall's future undecided while acting on other locations, and roughly 200 regular tennis users have organized opposition to the conversion per a 2023 report. Free, walk-up, no reservations.

Same pilot program as Hall Memorial Park — started August 2022, striping still in place today — but converted from underused handball courts rather than contested tennis courts, and pickleball has priority here at all times rather than sharing lines. No organized opposition to this conversion turned up in any source checked, a notably quieter story than Hall's. Free, walk-up, no reservations; exact court hours aren't published, only general 6am–10pm park hours.

Pickleball here is one sport among several in the Sports Center's Drop-In Sports/Open Gym program — first-come-first-served with no guaranteed spot, and participants must be high-school age or older. The exact weekly schedule is reported inconsistently across sources, and the city's own page doesn't publish specific times — call 408-586-3225 to confirm before going. Cost is $11 drop-in ($8 fee plus $3 processing) or covered by a Sports Center membership; other sources describe a separate $49/year membership option, so pricing may vary. These are temporary court markings on a general-purpose gym floor, not dedicated pickleball construction.

Milpitas' one dedicated indoor pickleball court, tucked inside a table tennis club — reservation only, $60 for a 2-hour block (2-hour minimum), up to 8 players, no drop-in or open play. Pickleheads separately lists an "Indoor Private Pickleball court" at this same address with matching pricing and description; the two listings are almost certainly the same physical court split into two entries, so we merged them into one record here rather than double-counting. The entrance is reportedly unmarked, with a keypad code sent by text on booking. Wheelchair accessible, with trainers and lessons available.

Opened July 10, 2026 — the day before this page was researched — so some details haven't fully settled. There's a genuine, unresolved conflict on whether this is indoor or outdoor: the club's own website's title tag calls it "Indoor Pickleball Courts," and an independent court finder and local news coverage both describe it as fully indoor, but that same club page also lists "Outdoor Surfacing" as an amenity with no further explanation — we're not picking a side here. Membership runs $199/month (after a $1 deposit) or $1,990/year; Founder membership tiers 2 and 3 sold out before doors opened. Locker rooms and showers are marked "TBD" on the club's own site as of this writing.

  • Hall Memorial Park and Peter Gill Memorial Park both run on the same "temporary" city pilot program that started in August 2022. Hall's conversion remains politically undecided as of the most recent report found (May 2025); Gill's has drawn no organized opposition.
  • The Picklr opened July 10, 2026, the day before this research — its own site lists "Outdoor Surfacing" even though its title tag and every other source call it indoor. Unresolved; call ahead or email milpitas.ca@thepicklrfranchise.com before a special trip.
  • Pickleheads' own citywide total (5 locations, 16 courts) likely double-counts one venue — "Indoor Private Pickleball court" and Silicon Valley Table Tennis and Pickleball share an address, price, and description. We merged them and added The Picklr, which isn't on Pickleheads at all, so this page's count — 5 venues, 31 courts — is a different set of 5 than Pickleheads' own.
  • Only Silicon Valley Table Tennis and Pickleball and The Picklr take reservations — Hall Memorial Park, Peter Gill Memorial Park, and Milpitas Sports Center are walk-up/drop-in only.
