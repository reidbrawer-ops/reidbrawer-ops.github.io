South Bay region

San Jose

The biggest pickleball scene in the Bay Area, though sources disagree on size — the San Jose Pickleball Club counts 24 locations (~98 courts), one aggregator says 25 locations and 109 courts, another counts 22. This guide covers 15+ verified venues: free city parks, a college campus, a membership club, and two indoor drop-in sessions.

22–25 locations (sources disagree) ~98–109 courts citywide Most free, no reservation

Where to play

Six dedicated concrete courts with permanent lines and portable nets, free and first-come-first-served. Lights mean evening play is easy to find, and it's the city's best-known, most active public court.

Eight dedicated acrylic courts, free to walk in and play. Renting a court for exclusive use runs through Facilitron. *Hours differ by source — roughly weekdays 6–9pm, weekends 8am–9pm — confirm before a special trip.

  • No San Jose city park sets an official skill-level restriction for open/free play — "beginner nights" and level tags are club-run sessions layered on top. Commercial venues like Ace Pickleball Club and Paddle Party do run tiered skill divisions.
  • Almaden Valley Athletic Club and the indoor community centers are exceptions to San Jose's free-and-open norm — expect fees or membership there.
  • San Jose Swim & Racquet Club, Silver Creek Valley Country Club, and Cottle Road Community Center came up in research but are left off — each was single-source or had unclear public access.
