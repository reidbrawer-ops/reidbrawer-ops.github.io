Cities / South Bay / San Jose

San Jose

The biggest pickleball scene in the Bay Area — the citywide tally lands somewhere around 22–25 locations and roughly 98–109 courts, depending on how you count satellite sites. This guide covers 15+ verified venues: free city parks, a college campus, a membership club, and two indoor drop-in sessions.

Six dedicated concrete courts with permanent lines and portable nets, free and first-come-first-served. Lights mean evening play is easy to find, and it's the city's best-known, most active public court.

Eight dedicated acrylic courts, free to walk in and play — not reservable for open play. Open play runs Mon–Fri 9am–12pm and 4–10pm, weekends 8am–10pm. A college pickleball class holds 4 of the 8 courts Tue/Thu 9–10:45am (the other 4 stay open to the public); mornings are beginner-friendly, traffic and skill level ramp up starting at 4pm on weekdays. Renting a court for exclusive use runs through Facilitron.

  • No San Jose city park sets an official skill-level restriction for open/free play — "beginner nights" and level tags are club-run sessions layered on top. Commercial venues like Ace Pickleball Club and Paddle Party do run tiered skill divisions.
  • Almaden Valley Athletic Club and the indoor community centers are exceptions to San Jose's free-and-open norm — expect fees or membership there.
  • San Jose Swim & Racquet Club, Silver Creek Valley Country Club, and Cottle Road Community Center came up in research but are left off — each was thinly documented or had unclear public access.
