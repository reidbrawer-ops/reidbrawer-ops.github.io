Berkeley

Berkeley's flagship pickleball venue is Cedar Rose Park — four dedicated outdoor courts, online booking through the city, and by a wide margin the busiest, most-cited place to play in town. James Kenney Park backs it up with a single lighted court closer to downtown. A couple of other parks get mentioned informally, but the city's own facilities list doesn't back them up yet.

Berkeley's real flagship court, and one the city's own directory previously missed. Four dedicated outdoor hard courts with permanent lines and nets: three bookable through the city's CivicRec system in up to 2-hour blocks between 8am and 8pm, plus a fourth court (#4) that's community-described as a free walk-up option, commonly said to open up "after 2pm" — that specific timing is a repeated community claim rather than something confirmed word-for-word in an official document, so treat it as a good guess rather than a guarantee. Cost is $7/hour for residents, $8/hour for non-residents, with reduced rates for youth, seniors, and people with disabilities. Court hours are deliberately narrower than the park's general 6am–10pm gate hours, run "in consideration of neighbors." On-site: wheelchair accessibility, restrooms, a water fountain, and quieter balls for noise mitigation. Six-plus independent sources describe this as one of the most popular places to play pickleball in Berkeley.

One dedicated outdoor hard court with a permanent net and lines, lighted for evening play, plus a practice wall — a nice extra if you're working on your own before a game shows up. Berkeley's own materials are inconsistent about whether this court is reservable or walk-up only; some sources describe it as bookable, others call it drop-in. Worth a call to confirm before you plan around a specific time slot.

Two more spots — reported informally, not on the city's official list

Grove Park and San Pablo Park both get mentioned as pickleball spots in community sources, but the City of Berkeley's own facilities page lists both as tennis-only court inventory, with no pickleball listed at either. That doesn't mean nothing ever happens there — informal or scheduled open play can exist without making it onto an official court list — but it does mean you shouldn't treat these as confirmed the way Cedar Rose Park and James Kenney Park are. Call ahead before a special trip.
