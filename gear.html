Gear & rentals

Don't own a paddle? Here's where to get one.

For travelers and beginners without their own equipment — real pro shops, demo programs, and actual rental counters, organized by the same 5 regions as the rest of this site. Every entry below was checked against the shop's own site or listing this pass; where we couldn't confirm something, we say so instead of guessing.

5 regions 15+ verified options $10–40 typical rental

Quick answer, if you just want to try it once

Sports Basement runs a rental counter at most of its Bay Area stores — Presidio (SF), Redwood City, Sunnyvale, Campbell, Berkeley, Walnut Creek, and Novato — with actual pickleball paddles you can rent for roughly $10–40 depending on the model, often credited toward a purchase if you buy within 30 days. The primary renter needs to show up with photo ID and a physical credit card. It's the closest thing on the Bay Area to a no-commitment "just let me try this" option, and it reaches 4 of the 5 regions below.

Why this region is short

Flyte Racquet Club in San Rafael — already covered on this site's San Rafael page for its 23 courts — has no pro shop, paddle sales, or rentals as of this pass; we checked their site directly. There are reports of informal "Royal Pickleball" ambassador demo tables showing up at San Rafael's Municipal Tennis and Pickleball Center and in Novato, but that looks like a seasonal volunteer program rather than a reliable storefront — don't plan a trip around it without calling first. The nearest Dick's Sporting Goods is in Petaluma, which is a real stretch to call North Bay in this site's sense, so it's not listed here as an option.

Good to know

  • Pickleball paddles are solid-faced and are never "strung" — that's a tennis-racquet term. If a shop advertises stringing services, that's for their tennis side, not your paddle.
  • The real pickleball-specific service most of these shops offer is grip replacement or edge-guard tape — not a full restring.
  • A cracked face or peeling edge guard isn't fixable at any Bay Area storefront we found. The realistic option is mail-in repair — services like PaddleFix (Gilbert, AZ) or D-Ball/Val-Paddles run roughly $20–45 plus about $6 shipping.
  • Demo programs (Palo Alto Tennis Shop, Mriva Sports, Courtside Tennis & Pickleball) all work the same way: a refundable deposit, a few weeks to try paddles, credited back if you buy. Rental counters (Sports Basement) are simpler — pay per rental, no deposit, sometimes credited toward a purchase.
