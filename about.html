About

A directory, not a marketplace.

Who runs this

Pickleball Bay Area is built and kept up by one Bay Area player who got tired of stale listings and app data that hadn't been checked in years. No investors, no ad sales team — just one person who wanted a directory they'd trust with their own Saturday morning plans.

How verification works

City rec pages come first: where a city publishes its own parks & recreation listing or facility directory, that's what a venue is checked against — not scraped from a crowdsourced app and left to rot. Not every venue has one. Private clubs and commercial facilities aren't in any city's directory, so those we check against the venue's own listing, and we cross-check community sources like Pickleheads, PicklePlay, DailyPB, and local clubs for what cities don't publish. Where sources disagree, or a city's own listing is vague, thin, or contradicts itself, we say so instead of papering over it.

Coverage

Every Bay Area city we can verify, from a full scene to a single park. A city with one park gets a short page — that's the whole scene, not a gap.

Independence

No court, city, or club can pay for placement, a higher rank, or a better badge. The only paid links on this site are paddle and gear links, and every one is disclosed as an affiliate link right where it appears — see the affiliate disclosure for the full policy.

Corrections

Courts change — a city adds lights, a fee goes up, a shared tennis court gets dedicated lines. If something here is wrong, tell us and we'll check it against the source and fix it.

How we rank courts

Every venue with more than one nearby alternative is rated on four things. (That's our editorial rating — separate from the community rankings, where players vote on six factors of their own.)

Surface

Dedicated pickleball lines on a good hard or acrylic surface rate highest, shared tennis/pickleball lines rate the middle, taped-on portable lines rate lowest.

Level of play

A range, not a score, based on what the venue publishes about who shows up — clinics, rated open play, casual drop-in.

Weather

Bay Area microclimate, not a forecast — coastal courts run foggy and windy, inland courts run hot and sunny.

Wait time

Based on reported popularity versus court count. Low means a short wait; high means bring a book.

Each city's Top pick highlights one confirmed court as a starting point — never a paid placement. As players rate courts on /rankings, it shifts to the highest-rated one, so it can move. Venues flagged "call ahead" aren't eligible.

Before you drive out

Hours, fees, and reservation systems change with city budgets and construction. Check the source link at the bottom of each city's page before making a special trip.
