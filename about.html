About

About

An independent field guide, not a court finder.

Bay Area cities, checked by hand against each city's own recreation department page — plus the local context an app won't give you, like which cities are still fighting over where to put their first court.

Sourcing

City rec pages first, cross-checked against Pickleheads, PicklePlay, DailyPB, and local clubs for what cities don't publish. Where sources disagree, we say so.

Scope

No ads, no sponsored placements, no pay-to-list. Our court data stays independent — no private club schedules or anything that needs an account to verify. Some paddle & gear links are affiliate links that may earn us a commission; that never affects which venues we list or how they rank. How this works.

Coverage

Every Bay Area city we can verify, from a full scene to a single park. A city with one park gets a short page — that's the whole scene, not a gap.

How we rank courts

Every venue with more than one nearby alternative is rated on four things.

Surface

Dedicated pickleball lines on a good hard or acrylic surface rate highest, shared tennis/pickleball lines rate the middle, taped-on portable lines rate lowest.

Level of play

A range, not a score, based on what the venue publishes about who shows up — clinics, rated open play, casual drop-in.

Weather

Bay Area microclimate, not a forecast — coastal courts run foggy and windy, inland courts run hot and sunny.

Wait time

Based on reported popularity versus court count. Low means a short wait; high means bring a book.

Each city's Top pick badge comes from community voting on /rankings, not a judgment call — vote and it can move. Venues flagged "call ahead" aren't eligible.

Before you drive out

Hours, fees, and reservation systems change with city budgets and construction. Check the source link at the bottom of each city's page before making a special trip.
